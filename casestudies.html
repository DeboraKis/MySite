Guiding Colleagues Through Training & Education

Challenge: As a Dental Nurse, I often worked in busy, high-pressure clinics where new staff and trainees needed to quickly get up to speed on both clinical processes and patient communication. Many were unfamiliar with protocols, which risked slowing down the patient journey and creating uncertainty within the team.

Action: I took initiative to guide new colleagues step by step through procedures, explaining not just the “how” but also the “why.” For example, when introducing infection control workflows, I created a simple, structured walkthrough they could follow during live practice. I encouraged questions, gave feedback in real time, and checked in regularly to ensure they felt confident and supported.

Result: Colleagues consistently reported feeling more confident after shadowing and working with me, which helped them settle into their roles faster. Our team was able to maintain efficiency and deliver a consistently high standard of care. This experience strengthened my ability to educate professionals in a clear, structured way while building trust and engagement, skills I now bring into sales and onboarding contexts.

Key Skills: Education & training · Relationship building · Clear communication · Confidence-building

Managing Patients and Stakeholders with Care

Challenge: As both a Dental Nurse and Receptionist, I was often the first point of contact for patients, many of whom were anxious, confused about treatments, or uncertain about next steps. At the same time, I had to coordinate with dentists and specialists to keep the clinic running smoothly.

Action: I developed a patient-first approach: listening carefully, explaining treatment options in straightforward language, and proactively managing expectations around costs, timings, and outcomes. On the operational side, I managed bookings, prioritised urgent cases, and communicated directly with multiple clinicians to balance their schedules.

Result: Patients frequently expressed relief and gratitude for having their concerns explained clearly and calmly, which directly improved satisfaction and trust in the clinic. Dentists relied on me to keep schedules full and organised, ensuring the business ran efficiently. These experiences taught me how to convert initial interest into long-term trust and loyalty by combining empathy with clear, solution-focused communication.

Key Skills: Customer relationship management · Scheduling & coordination · Empathetic communication · Trust-building

Leadership & Management in Busy Clinics

Challenge: In fast-paced dental clinics, ensuring smooth coordination between multiple dentists, specialists, and support staff required both strong leadership and adaptability. Without clear direction, miscommunication and delays could impact both team efficiency and patient satisfaction.

Action: I took ownership of daily workflows, delegating tasks where necessary and making quick decisions under pressure. I often acted as the bridge between clinical and administrative teams, ensuring priorities were aligned. I motivated colleagues by leading with clarity, supporting them during challenges, and stepping in where extra guidance was needed.

Result: The clinics I supported ran more efficiently, with smoother team coordination and faster resolution of unexpected challenges. Dentists and managers often relied on me to take initiative in high-pressure moments, which demonstrated my ability to lead while fostering a collaborative environment. These experiences strengthened my leadership and management skills, which I continue to build on in professional contexts.

Key Skills: Team leadership · Task delegation · Operational management · Decision-making under pressure
